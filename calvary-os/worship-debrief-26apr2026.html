General observation
Structure is the through-line 🧭
The biggest single thread running through this week's gaps: the agreed structure of songs didn't fully hold up in the room. The Lord You Are Good repeats. The Friend of God ending. The Trading My Sorrows → Afro switch that came too late. Three different songs, one underlying theme.

The fix isn't about one person holding the structure for everyone — it's about all of us knowing it cold. See the structure section below for how we lean into this.

What we're working on
Song structure — Lord You Are Good
The agreed structure from rehearsal didn't get followed cleanly. Mid-song, Uncle Caster wasn't sure how many verse/chorus repeats and the band ended up guessing where he was going. At one point part of the band heard a "you are good" cue and started playing the bridge while others continued the verse. The fix is everyone knowing the agreed structure cold — so when one person drifts, the rest of the band stays anchored and the structure holds.
Friend of God ending
This was the messiest moment of the set. Number of rounds wasn't agreed in the moment, count was lost — some played four rounds, some three, some two. It was visible. As Uncle Mike put it: most songs you don't sing a section an odd number of times because the band most likely will miss the cue. The fix: end on an even number, commit to the ending, and if you miss the cue you miss it — but stop drifting.
Trading My Sorrows → Afro switch
The plan was to switch to Afro on the fourth repeat. By the time Uncle Caster called "sing it one more time", the switch hadn't happened — too late then to change style. The fix is everyone knowing exactly when the switch lands so it doesn't depend on one person remembering at the right moment. Lock it in rehearsal, hold it in service.
Toms — pull back on the build
Coming in too early on the toms during builds — specifically the "God Almighty" section in Friend of God. That section needs to build: pull the toms back, let it grow, then bring them in. When Laolu was in time he was in sync and added well — the note is about restraint on the build, not about the playing.
Volume balance — keys + bass
Keys were too loud at points. The mixer couldn't be accessed from the floor — too many devices connected, only one person can control the desk at a time with the current setup. Bass was quiet because Bolaji A had to switch to passive and the gain bump didn't fully compensate. The principle: agree before service who has the desk, so any tweaks during the set are intentional and don't surprise the team.
Morayo — playing styles
Heard clearly during Friend of God when playing variations, but drowned out elsewhere — keys are also harmonically strings and will swallow chord-based guitar. Coaching from Uncle Mike: most worship songs are 1-4-5. Watch rock and Congolese guitarists — learn typical solos. They're usually just 1-4-5 played fancifully. Once you've got a handful of patterns, you can drop them into almost any song or section as flavour. Not overcomplicated. Just adding something other than chords.
On structure 🎙️

Structure was the recurring theme of today. Three songs, one issue. The thing BJ and I have been talking about is this: everyone needs to know their parts, and that means rehearsing in your own time — listening back to the rehearsal recording, reviewing the notes, locking it in. Service time is short. The window for things to go right is tight. The more we know cold going in, the less we have to figure out in the room.

This is also why we're staying with similar sets week-on-week. The reason we could fix the transition issue from last week is that we played the same Praise set — repetition gave us the runway to actually correct what we'd identified. We do the same with structure. Lock it down, run it again, and the muscle memory builds. That investment compounds.

Uncle Mike shared a complementary view at the debrief — worth holding alongside this:
