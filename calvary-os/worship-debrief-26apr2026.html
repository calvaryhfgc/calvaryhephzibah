General observation
The cues need a voice 🎙️
The biggest single thread running through this week's gaps: cues that lived inside one person's head and didn't make it to the rest of the band. The Lord You Are Good structure confusion. The Friend of God ending. The Trading My Sorrows → Afro switch that came too late. Three different problems, one underlying cause.

The fix isn't more rehearsal. It's vocalising the cue out loud in the moment. The MD section below covers this in detail.

What we're working on
Song structure — Lord You Are Good
The agreed structure from rehearsal didn't get followed cleanly. Mid-song, Caster wasn't sure how many verse/chorus repeats and the band ended up guessing where he was going. At one point part of the band heard a "you are good" cue and started playing the bridge while others continued the verse. The fix is the MD voice (next section) — vocalising the structure call out loud in the moment.
Friend of God ending
This was the messiest moment of the set. Number of rounds wasn't agreed in the moment, count was lost — some played four rounds, some three, some two. It was visible. As Uncle Mike put it: most songs you don't sing a section an odd number of times because the band most likely will miss the cue. The fix: end on an even number, commit to the ending, and if you miss the cue you miss it — but stop drifting.
Trading My Sorrows → Afro switch
The plan was to switch to Afro on the fourth repeat. By the time Caster called "sing it one more time", the switch hadn't happened — too late then to change style. If Laolu has the cue in his head, he vocalises it at the right moment so the rest of the band knows. Don't keep the cue silent.
Toms — pull back on the build
Coming in too early on the toms during builds — specifically the "God Almighty" section in Friend of God. That section needs to build: pull the toms back, let it grow, then bring them in. When Laolu was in time he was in sync and added well — the note is about restraint on the build, not about the playing.
Volume balance — keys + bass
Keys were too loud at points. The mixer couldn't be accessed from the floor — too many devices connected, only one person can control the desk at a time with the current setup. Bass was quiet because Bolaji A had to switch to passive and the gain bump didn't fully compensate. The principle: the MD on a given Sunday has the desk; if anyone else is going to tweak from the floor, it gets agreed before service rather than improvised.
Morayo — playing styles
Heard clearly during Friend of God when playing variations, but drowned out elsewhere — keys are also harmonically strings and will swallow chord-based guitar. Coaching from Uncle Mike: most worship songs are 1-4-5. Watch rock and Congolese guitarists — learn typical solos. They're usually just 1-4-5 played fancifully. Once you've got a handful of patterns, you can drop them into almost any song or section as flavour. Not overcomplicated. Just adding something other than chords.
Esther — projection & confidence
Mic quiet at the start. The note isn't about technique alone — there's an element of fear which is completely understandable. The work is projecting from the diaphragm rather than the throat. This is teachable. Dad can help, and we'll find some videos to support. Other than that, doing really well — it's a confidence thing.
Caster — enunciation
English is Caster's third language, so words like magnified and apostrophe contractions like God's are genuinely hard. God's tends to come out as God is; magnified gets weighted on the wrong syllables. As Uncle Mike put it — easily solvable, teachable. Worth a small bit of focused work between Sundays on the lyric sheets where these specific words appear.
The MD role 🎙️
From Uncle Mike
→
One MD per service. Vocalised, not implicit. Church is fluid — the worship leader will make in-the-moment calls. The MD's job is to say it out loud so everyone else hears the call.
→
"Verse, verse, chorus, chorus." "We're looping." "Switch to Afro." "This is the last round." The cue in your head doesn't help the band. Vocalise it.
→
The same principle applies to the desk. The MD has the mixer. If anyone else is going to control sound from the floor, that's agreed before service — not improvised mid-set.
→
Be assertive. We miss the end sometimes. It happens. But commit to the call.
