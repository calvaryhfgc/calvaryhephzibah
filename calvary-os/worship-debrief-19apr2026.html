What we're working on
Key transition
Lord You Are Good → Friend of God (B to E)
We got there — and fair play for making it work on the day — but this is the one thing to specifically rehearse before next Sunday. The B to E key change needs a deliberate plan: a walkdown through the circle of fifths is the move. Once we practise it properly it'll feel natural. It's very solvable.
Dynamics
There's room to develop our dynamics as a team
Overall the energy was great — but there are moments where letting the song breathe a little longer before building would add even more impact. The principle: give the congregation time to settle into a song, then build. When the full band comes in they'll feel it more. This is something we grow into together as we rehearse more.
The mix
Room to improve the overall sound mix — but the stream was the best it's been in a long time
There's always room to tighten the mix and we'll keep working on that. But it's worth noting — the stream recording was the best we've had in a long time. That's a real win and it matters for everyone watching online.
Guitar vs Keys distinction
Guitar and keys sounding too similar in the mix
At times the guitar and keys were blending into each other — hard to tell them apart. Eddie and Morayo, let's talk tones before next Sunday so each instrument has its own space in the mix. Again, very fixable.
Looking ahead
