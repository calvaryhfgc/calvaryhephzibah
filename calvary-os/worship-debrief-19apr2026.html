What to improve
Key transition
Lord You Are Good → Friend of God (B to E)
The transition happened — they got there in the end — but it was acknowledged as awkward. The key jump from B to E is non-trivial and needs deliberate rehearsal. Suggestions included walking the bass down through the circle of fifths to make the change feel more musical.
"We didn't nail the Friend of God transition. We got there in the end but it was a bit awkward."— Team
"You could walk it down. B to E — it's just down here via the circle. Give everyone time to walk into it."— Team discussion
Drums — dynamics and space
Laolu came in too early on some worship songs
On Here I Am to Worship specifically, Laolu came in after only one run of the verse — before the atmosphere had built. The note: let it breathe first. Come in reserved, build gradually. The verse should be run at least twice before drums come in fully.
"There has to be at least twice. If you come in, come in reserved — don't come in playing your normal. Give me half of that, then build."— Bolaji
Guitar style
Morayo needs to develop melodic playing alongside rhythm
Morayo's rhythm playing is solid but her style hasn't developed much over the past year. The feedback: learn to play melodically, especially during worship songs. A few melodic keys lines on top of the rhythm will add atmosphere and help distinguish her sound in the mix.
"You haven't changed your style. You're better but stylistically it's the same. Learn to play the melody — it adds a layer, adds atmosphere."— Bolaji
Bass levels
Bass was too loud in the mix
Bolaji A's bass was noted as too loud at points. A sound design conversation is needed — the overall mix needs work and this is a solvable technical issue.
"The bass was loud. We need to turn it down."— Team
Guitar vs Keys distinction
Difficult to distinguish guitar from keys in the mix
The timbre of guitar and keys were too similar — hard to tell them apart. Eddie was asked to try different tones in the moment. This is a sound design issue to address before the next service.
"The tambre of the sound is very difficult to distinguish which is which — you need to know when it's you and when it's Morayo."— Bolaji
Strategic notes — from private debrief
Singers are now the gap
Vocal consistency is the next priority
My Jesus (Shout to the Lord) was removed from the set because the key was too high for Vanessa. The singers situation needs to be resolved — more options, more rehearsal time. Tosin Akindele's longer-term training partnership becomes critical here.
Rehearsal frequency
Need to rehearse more regularly — not just before Sundays
Current pattern is effectively figuring it out on the day. This works with experienced musicians but isn't sustainable and limits growth. Regular mid-week rehearsals needed.
"We have to rehearse more often. It's not always together — we're kind of figuring it out on the day."— Pastor Shade
Bolaji Adebanjo development
Need to make a leader out of him
Bolaji A is described as very musical and technically good. The challenge is developing him into a worship leader/MD, not just a session musician. Bolaji (CoS) is working closely with him on this.
"He's very musical, knows his stuff — but we need to make a leader out of him. That's the challenge."— Pastor Shade
