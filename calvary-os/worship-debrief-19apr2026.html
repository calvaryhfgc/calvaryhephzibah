What went well
Congregation didn't need the screen
The greatest hits approach landed exactly as we hoped. We stripped back to the parts everyone knows — and people just sang. Loud. Nobody missed the sections we cut. That's the brief done right there.
High energy maintained throughout
From Trading My Sorrows to the close — the energy never really dropped. It felt like a lift the whole way through. Even the quieter worship moments felt intentional, not flat. That's a hard thing to pull off and we did it.
Communion moment was pitched perfectly
The worship pulled back at exactly the right moment — soft enough to hold space for communion without losing the room. Really well read by everyone.
Everyone was singing
The whole church was singing together. Older members, younger members — everyone knew where they were. That doesn't always happen and it's a really good sign.
Worship section flowed beautifully
The transitions between Here I Am to Worship, What a Beautiful Name and I Exalt Thee were smooth and connected. The worship section felt like one continuous moment rather than separate songs. That's exactly what we want.
The stream was the best it's been in a long time
For everyone watching online — the recording quality was noticeably better than we've had recently. That's a real win and it matters.

General observations
It took a moment to find our feet
If we're being honest — the first song had a slightly rocky start. We were feeling our way into it and only really hit our stride around verse 2. But when we got there? It was a real vibe. The lesson: we need a plan for how we open. The first 60 seconds sets the tone for everything that follows.
Strategy — repetition over rotation 🔁
This is something Uncle B has been discussing with Bolaji A and it's now the direction — rather than changing the set week on week, we're going to aim to perfect one set over the next 4 weeks or so. The Praise section stays largely the same. Worship may have some variation. But the core songs stay consistent.

The reason is simple: with all the moving parts we have right now, consistency is what builds tightness. It's hard to measure progress if the songs keep changing. Our measure of success going forward is how tight did we get the core songs — not how many new songs we introduced. We want to know this set like the back of our hand.

What we're working on
Transitions
Transitions are the one area to keep working on. The fix isn't complicated — it's repetition. The more we play the same set together, the tighter the transitions get. This is exactly why regular rehearsals matter and why sticking with the same set for a period of time is the plan.
Dynamics as a team
There are moments where letting a song breathe a little longer before building would add even more impact. Give the congregation time to settle in, then build. When the full band comes in they'll feel it even more. This is something we grow into together.
The mix
There's always room to tighten the overall mix and we'll keep working on that — but as mentioned above, the stream was the best it's been in a long time. Good foundation to build from.
