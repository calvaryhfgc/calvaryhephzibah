What we're working on
The mix — couldn't hear ourselves
Uncle Caster lost the click at points. Uncle B on second keys couldn't hear his own playing clearly enough to know whether he was hitting the right notes — "I hope I'm playing the right note, because I have no idea what it sounds like." The room was loud, the monitor mix was thin, and turning individual volumes up would have drowned the rest. We need a usable monitor mix per player.
Esther — warm-ups happen at home
Small habit. The vocal warm-up needs to happen before Sunday, not on the day. A light limber-up when you arrive is fine; the actual work happens during the week.
Volume balance — second keys
Second keys weren't loud enough in mix-one — same root cause as the click issue. Once the desk is consistently usable mid-service, this becomes a tweak rather than a structural problem.
Setup & the layer behind the layer 🛠️

Setup ran over thirty minutes long. The band didn't start running through until 10:10 instead of being done by 9:30. Adding a second key, the wedge and a couple of extra connections shouldn't have cost us forty minutes. We need to figure out what specifically slowed us down.

Two bigger truths surfaced underneath that:

1. Bolaji A is a single point of failure on the desk
The only person who really knows how to use the mixer is Bolaji A. When he's playing, no one can adjust the mix. When something needs fixing mid-service he has to stop what he's doing to walk someone through it. That isn't sustainable. The fix is teaching others.
2. The team behind the team needs growing
Noah and Tarell are sitting in roles where, with a small amount of training, they could carry real responsibility on sound and tech. Bolaji A's instinct to hold on is understandable — the pressure to do it right is real — but we won't grow if we don't give people stuff to do.
