General observation
The musical bottleneck has moved 🛠️
For three weeks the conversation has been about playing — transitions, structure, song endings. Today the conversation moved. The band played well; what tripped us up was setup, mixing access and the absence of a layer of people behind the main team. That's a different kind of problem and it needs a different kind of work. See the setup section below.
What we're working on
The mix — couldn't hear the click
Caster lost the track at points. Bolaji A on keys couldn't hear his own notes clearly enough to know whether he was playing the right thing — "I hope I'm playing the right note, because I have no idea what it sounds like." The room was loud, the monitor mix was thin, and turning his own volume up would have drowned the rest. We need a usable monitor mix per player — not a guess.
Esther — warm-ups happen at home
Small habit, big difference. The vocal warm-up needs to happen before Sunday — not on the day. A light limber-up when you arrive is fine; the actual work happens during the week. Get this into your routine and your confidence on the day grows with it.
Volume balance — second keys under in the mix
Bolaji A's keys weren't sufficiently audible in the mix-one feed. Same root cause as the click issue — the monitoring and balance setup needs review. Once the desk is consistently usable mid-service (see below), this becomes a tweak rather than a structural problem.
Setup & the layer behind the layer 🛠️

This was the longest single thread of the debrief. Setup ran over thirty minutes long — the band didn't start running through until 10:10 instead of being done by 9:30. Adding a second key, the wedge and a couple of extra connections shouldn't have cost us 40 minutes, but it did. We need to figure out why.

Two bigger truths surfaced underneath that:

1. Bolaji A is the single point of failure on the desk
Right now, the only person who really knows how to use the mixer is Bolaji A. Which means when he's playing, no one can adjust the mix — and when something needs fixing mid-service he has to stop what he's doing to walk someone through it. That's not a sustainable setup. The fix isn't to hold tighter to the desk; it's to teach others.
2. The team behind the team needs growing
Noah and Tarell are sitting in roles where, with a small amount of training, they could carry real responsibility on sound and tech. We're not going to grow if we don't give people stuff to do. The lesson Uncle B has lived for years on the media side — start small, expect imperfection, give it weeks to bed in — is the same lesson we need to apply now to sound and setup.

Looking ahead
Same set, again — but tighter
Repetition continues to compound. The set is mostly held; small tweaks land each week. Worship may flex slightly but the core stays. The discipline is to keep doing the same thing slightly better, week on week, rather than chase novelty.
Earlier setup when there's extra to do
If a Sunday has anything beyond the standard setup — a second key, an extra wedge, new connections — flag it in the week and pull setup forward by 15 minutes. Three of us coming in earlier is enough; we don't need everyone there. That buffer is what keeps us out of a panic at 10:10.
Start training Noah & Tarell on the desk
Even small. Give them one thing to own — say, a single channel or a specific cue — and let them learn it. They listen to music. They know what a good mix sounds like. Once the screen is in front of them and the basics are explained, they'll surprise us. This is week one of what's likely a six-to-eight-week curve.
Build the kit list
Two lists. Ideal setup. Right-now needs. The right-now list is the priority for funding and procurement. Comms kit, IEMs, singer monitors top the list. This goes via Pastor Shade for budget approval.
Continue growing Esther 💐
Small things — warmups during the week, more reps, more confidence. Keep creating space for her to develop. Today was a strong starting point.
