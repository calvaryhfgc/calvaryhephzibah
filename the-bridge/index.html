Worship rebuild
An opportunity to build it right. Five phases, clear principles.
Foundation principles
Identity over personality

Worship belongs to Calvary, not any individual. Sound, values, culture — defined by the mission.

Develop, don't just recruit

Look inside first. Gifts in the congregation haven't had room to grow. Create that room.

Shared leadership

No single point of failure. Build 3–4 people who can each lead confidently. Rotate.

Serve the sermon

Worship and word in alignment. The team should know the theme before Sunday.

The five phases
1
Stabilise — appoint an interim coordinator
Someone trustworthy holds things together week-to-week while the longer plan is built. Likely an existing team member.
Now — within 2 weeks
2
Audit — who do we actually have?
Quietly map every musician, vocalist, and gifted person. Not an open call — just internal knowledge. Who stayed? Who's never been activated?
Weeks 2–4
3
Define the vision — what does Calvary worship sound like?
Pastor Shade leads a small private conversation with 2–3 trusted people. This shapes everything: repertoire, style, who leads.
Weeks 3–5
4
Invite and form the new team
Personal invitations only. Start with 4–6 people. The first gathering is spiritual, not logistical. Prayer and vision before rotas.
Weeks 5–8
5
Rehearse, develop, and commission
Intentional development — character, commitment, theology of worship. Commission publicly. Make it a moment for the whole church.

Unofficial Chief of Staff. Most trusted person to the General Overseer. Across media, operations, hiring, and strategy. Family founder's son. The only person currently positioned to hold the coordination layer the Resident Pastor role left vacant.

What's holding you back

No formal title. Navigating carefully around a dysfunctional team. Reluctance to be seen as stepping on toes. But the window is now — the departures have created space, and the church needs someone to move decisively.

What you have that no one else does

Pastor Shade's trust. Family mandate. Strategic clarity. The systems. And the willingness to build rather than just talk. That combination is rare and it matters.

The conversation that needs to happen
