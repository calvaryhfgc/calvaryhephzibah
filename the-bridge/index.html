Worship rebuild
An opportunity to build it right. Five phases, four principles.
Foundation principles
Identity over personality

Worship belongs to Calvary, not any individual. Sound, values, culture — defined by the mission.

Develop, don't just recruit

Look inside first. Gifts in the congregation that haven't had room to grow. Create that room.

Shared leadership

No single point of failure. Build 3–4 people who can each lead confidently. Rotate.

Serve the sermon

Worship and word in alignment. The team should know the theme before Sunday.

Five phases
1
Stabilise — appoint an interim coordinator
Someone trustworthy holds things together week-to-week while the longer plan is built. Likely an existing team member. The congregation should not feel a gap.
Now — within 2 weeks
2
Audit — who do we actually have?
Quietly map every musician, vocalist, and gifted person. Not an open call — just internal knowledge. Who stayed? Who has never been activated?
Weeks 2–4
3
Define the vision — what does Calvary worship sound like?
Pastor Shade leads a small, private conversation with 2–3 trusted people. What is the spiritual intention? This shapes everything that follows.
Weeks 3–5
4
Invite and form the new team
Personal invitations only — not a general call. Start with 4–6 people. First gathering is spiritual before logistical. Vision before rotas.
Weeks 5–8
5
Rehearse, develop, and commission
Intentional development — character, commitment, theology of worship. Commission publicly. Make it a moment for the whole church.
Weeks 8–16
