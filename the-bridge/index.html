Worship rebuild
This is an opportunity, not a crisis. Build it right this time.
Foundation principles
Identity over personality

The worship belongs to Calvary, not to any individual. Sound, values, culture — defined by the mission.

Develop, don't just recruit

Look inside first. Gifts in the congregation that haven't had room to grow. Create that room.

Shared leadership

No single point of failure. Rotate worship leaders. Build 3–4 people who can each lead confidently.

Serve the sermon

Worship and word in alignment. The team should know the theme before Sunday. Co-ordination, not performance.

The five phases
1
Stabilise — appoint an interim coordinator
Someone trustworthy and present takes the week-to-week coordination. Not necessarily a worship leader — just someone who can hold things together. Likely an existing team member.
Now — within 2 weeks
2
Audit — who do we actually have?
Quietly identify every musician, vocalist, and gifted person in the congregation. Not an open call yet — just internal knowledge. Who stayed? Who has never been activated?
Weeks 2–4
3
Define the vision — what does Calvary worship sound like?
Pastor Shade leads a small, private conversation with 2–3 trusted people. What is the spiritual intention of worship at Calvary? This shapes everything that follows.
Weeks 3–5
4
Invite and form the new team
Personal invitations, not a general announcement. Start small — 4 to 6 people. The first gathering is spiritual, not logistical. Begin with prayer and vision before you talk about rotas.
Weeks 5–8
5
Rehearse, develop, and commission
Regular rehearsals with intentional development. Character, commitment, the theology of worship. Commission the team publicly when ready — make it a moment for the whole church.
