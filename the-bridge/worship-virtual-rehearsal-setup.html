Why we're doing this

Getting everyone in the same room for rehearsal is hard. People work shifts. People live far away. Some weeks we just can't make it work.

So we're going to rehearse together virtually — from our own homes, in real time.

"Zoom is great for talking. It's not built for music. The sound lag makes it impossible to play and sing in time together."

JamKazam is a tool built specifically for musicians playing together online. It cuts the lag right down, so we can actually worship and rehearse as one team — even when we're not in the same place.

📋 What you need before you start

Read this list before doing anything else. If you're missing something, message the group chat and we'll help.

💻
A laptop or desktop computer
Windows 10 or 11, or a Mac (macOS 10.15 or newer). It will NOT work on a phone or tablet. No iPhones, no iPads, no Android. This is the main thing.
🌐
A wired internet connection (Ethernet cable)
WiFi is too slow for live music. You need to plug your computer directly into your home router using an Ethernet cable. If your laptop doesn't have an Ethernet port, you'll need a small USB adapter — they're about £8 on Amazon.
🎧
Wired headphones
Any wired headphones will do to start. Bluetooth/wireless headphones add lag — don't use them.
🎤
A microphone
Your laptop's built-in mic is enough to get started. We can upgrade later.
🎸
Audio interface (only if you play an instrument)
If you play keys, guitar, bass or drums, you'll eventually need a small box called an audio interface to plug in properly. See the hardware section below — don't worry, vocalists can skip this.

Step 1 — Create your free JamKazam account

This takes about 2 minutes. Do it on the same computer you'll rehearse on.

1
Go to the JamKazam website

Open your web browser and go to www.jamkazam.com

2
Click the orange "Join Today, Play Free!" button

It's on the front page. You can't miss it.

3
Fill in your details

Name, email, password. Tick the box to agree to their terms. Tick the "I'm not a robot" box. Then click Create Account.

Tip: Use an email you actually check — they'll send you a confirmation link.
4
Check your email

Click the confirmation link they send you. That's your account ready.

Step 2 — Download the desktop app

JamKazam runs as a proper app on your computer, not in a browser tab.

1
Go to the downloads page

After you sign up, you should see a download button straight away. If not, go to www.jamkazam.com/downloads

2
Pick Windows or Mac

Click the right button for your computer. The installer file will download.

3
Open the installer and follow the prompts

On Windows: Double-click the file. Click Next, then Install. If Windows asks "Do you want this app to make changes?" — click Yes.

On Mac: Double-click the file. Drag the JamKazam icon onto the Applications folder icon. Then open it from your Applications folder.

If your Mac says "Can't be opened because it's from an unidentified developer": Right-click the app and choose Open. Then click Open again in the pop-up. You only have to do this once.
4
Sign in

Open the app and sign in with the email and password you just created. You should see your name in the top right corner — that means you're in.

Step 3 — Run the setup wizard

The first time you open the app, it walks you through some checks. Just follow along.

1
Plug in your Ethernet cable first

Connect your computer to your home router with the cable before running the wizard. Turn WiFi off on your computer so it doesn't accidentally use it.

2
Let it test your internet

The wizard will check your connection speed. This takes a couple of minutes. Don't open Netflix or YouTube while it runs — it needs your full bandwidth to test properly.

3
Set up your audio

Choose your microphone and your headphones from the drop-down menus. If you're using your laptop's built-in mic, pick that. If you've got an audio interface plugged in, pick that instead.

Tip: Plug your headphones in before opening the app, so JamKazam sees them straight away.
4
Test you can hear and be heard

The wizard plays a sound to check your headphones, then records you to check your mic. Speak or sing a few words. If you hear yourself back, you're good.

🎸 Hardware for instrumentalists

If you play an instrument, this section is for you. Vocalists — you can skip to the next part.

The short version: to plug an instrument into your computer properly, you need a small box called an audio interface. It sits between your instrument and your laptop, and it makes the sound clean and fast. Without one, your sound will be laggy and noisy.
Audio Interface
Everyone playing an instrument

A small USB box with inputs for your instrument and outputs for your headphones. Budget options that work well:

  • Behringer UMC22 — around £45. Cheapest option that still works properly.
  • Focusrite Scarlett Solo (4th Gen) — around £110. Industry standard, great sound.
  • PreSonus AudioBox USB 96 — around £80. Good middle ground.

Let Bolaji know before you buy — we may be able to source them through the church.

Instrument Cable (¼ inch)
Guitar, bass, electric keys

A standard guitar lead. One end goes into your instrument, the other goes into the audio interface. Most of us already have one.

Headphones with ¼ inch (6.3mm) jack
Anyone using an audio interface

This catches people out. Most audio interfaces use the big headphone plug, not the small one your phone uses. If your headphones have the small plug, you can buy a £3 adapter that converts it to the big size.

Microphone (XLR type)
Vocalists who want better sound later

You don't need this for our first session. Start with your laptop mic. When you're ready to upgrade, a Shure SM58 (~£100) is the church standard — plugs into the audio interface using an XLR cable.

Drummers — a note
Drums

Acoustic drums are tricky for virtual rehearsal because they're loud and hard to mic at home. For now, if you have an electronic kit, you can plug it straight into an audio interface. If you only have an acoustic kit, message BJ and we'll work out a plan together.

⚠️ Three things that will trip you up

Almost everyone hits these. Save yourself the headache:

  • WiFi will not work properly. You MUST use an Ethernet cable. WiFi adds enough lag that nothing will be in time.
  • Phones and tablets don't work. JamKazam only runs on Windows or Mac computers. iPad won't work. iPhone won't work. Android won't work.
  • Bluetooth headphones add lag. AirPods, wireless headphones — they'll throw your timing off. Use wired headphones only.
