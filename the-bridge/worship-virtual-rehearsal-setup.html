00 · Why This

Together,
Even When Apart.

Getting everyone in the same room for rehearsal is hard. People work shifts. People live far away. Some weeks we just can't make it work.

So we're going to start rehearsing virtually — from our own homes, in real time, as one team.

"Zoom is great for talking. It is not built for music. The sound lag makes it impossible to play and sing in time together."

JamKazam is built specifically for musicians playing together online. It cuts the lag right down, so we can actually worship and rehearse as a band — even when we're not in the same place.

01 · Before You Start

What You Need.

Read this list first. If you are missing something, message the team chat — we will sort it out together.

01
A Laptop or Desktop
Windows 10 or 11, or a Mac (macOS 10.15 or newer). This is the big one — JamKazam does not run on phones or tablets. No iPhone, no iPad, no Android.
Critical
02
A Wired Internet Connection
WiFi is too slow for live music. You need to plug your computer directly into your home router using an Ethernet cable. If your laptop has no Ethernet port, a small USB adapter does the job — about £8 on Amazon.
Critical
03
Wired Headphones
Any wired headphones will do. Bluetooth and AirPods add lag — leave them off the table.
Required
04
A Microphone
Your laptop's built-in mic is enough to start. We can upgrade later.
Required
05
Audio Interface
Only if you play an instrument. Vocalists can skip this. Full details further down.
Instrument

About two minutes. Do it on the same computer you will rehearse on.

Open your web browser and go to www.jamkazam.com

02

Name, email, password. Tick the box to agree to their terms. Tick the "I'm not a robot" box. Then click Create Account.

04
Confirm Your Email

Open the email JamKazam sent. Click the link. Account ready.

▸Mac — drag JamKazam onto the Applications folder

Open the app. Sign in with the email and password you just created. Look top-right — you should see your name. That means you are in.

04 · Step Three

Run the
Setup Wizard.

First time you open the app, it walks you through the checks. Just follow along.

01
Plug In the Ethernet Cable First

Connect your computer to your router with the cable before running the wizard. Turn WiFi off on your computer so it cannot fall back to it.

02
Let It Test Your Internet

The wizard checks your connection speed. This takes a couple of minutes. Don't run Netflix or YouTube while it tests — it needs all your bandwidth.

03
Choose Your Mic and Headphones

Pick them from the drop-downs. If you are using your laptop's built-in mic, pick that. If your audio interface is plugged in, pick that instead.

Tip: plug headphones in before opening the app, so JamKazam sees them straight away.
04
Test You Can Hear and Be Heard

The wizard plays a sound to check your headphones, then records you to check your mic. Sing or speak a few words. If you hear yourself back — you are good.

05 · Instrumentalists

The Gear.

If you play an instrument, this section is for you. Vocalists can skip ahead.

The short version: to plug an instrument into your computer properly, you need a small box called an audio interface. It sits between your instrument and your laptop. It makes the sound clean and fast. Without one, your sound will be laggy and noisy.

Audio Interface

All instrumentalists

A small USB box with inputs for your instrument and outputs for your headphones. Budget options that work:

  • Behringer UMC22 — around £45. Cheapest option that still works properly.
  • PreSonus AudioBox USB 96 — around £80. Solid middle ground.
  • Focusrite Scarlett Solo (4th Gen) — around £110. Industry standard, great sound.
Speak to Bolaji before buying — we may be able to source through the church.

Instrument Cable

Guitar · Bass · Electric Keys

A standard ¼-inch guitar lead. One end into your instrument, the other into the audio interface. Most of us already have one.

Headphones with ¼-inch Jack

Anyone using an interface

This catches people out. Most audio interfaces use the large headphone plug, not the small one your phone uses. If your headphones have the small plug, a £3 adapter converts it to the large size.

XLR Microphone

Vocalists upgrading later

You do not need this for our first session. Start with your laptop mic. When you upgrade, a Shure SM58 (around £100) is the church standard — plugs into the audio interface using an XLR cable.

Drummers — A Note

Drums

Acoustic drums are tricky for virtual rehearsal because they are loud and hard to mic at home. If you have an electronic kit, it plugs straight into an audio interface. If you only have acoustic — message BJ and we will work out a plan together.

06 · Watch Out For

Three Things
That Trip People Up.

Almost everyone hits at least one of these. Save yourself the headache.

01
WiFi Will Not Work

You must use an Ethernet cable. WiFi adds enough lag that nothing will be in time. There is no workaround.

02
Phones and Tablets Do Not Work

JamKazam only runs on Windows or Mac computers. No iPad. No iPhone. No Android. You need an actual laptop or desktop.

03
Bluetooth Headphones Add Lag

AirPods, wireless headphones — they throw your timing off. Wired only. Always.
