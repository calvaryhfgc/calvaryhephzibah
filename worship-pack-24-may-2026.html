02 · Band Discipline

How We Play

The Principles

  • Structure over spontaneityPraise medley is set · Worship breathes
  • Heavy intros, then drop outInstant dynamics, space for vocals
  • Drop dynamics in versesBuild into the chorus, every time
  • Definitive endingsRun up to the crash · don't just stop
  • Seamless transitionsNo count-ins between medley songs
  • Same chord progression, varied baselineVariation lives in dynamics and accents
  • No man left behindGroup pace = lowest common denominator
  • Greatest hits energyStewards into worship · 20 minutes is enough

01
These Are the Days of Elijah
Checking…
Structure · Donnie McClurkin groove · 105 BPMOpener for the medley. Slower than we normally play it. STRUCTURE • Intro: Chorus — heavy-handed, then drop out for instant dynamics • Verse 1 ("These are the days of Elijah") • Chorus ("Behold He comes") ×1 • Verse 2 ("These are the days of Ezekiel") • Chorus ×2 on the second pass • Vamp (2 cycles) → transitions into Lord I Lift Your Name on High KEY NOTES • SKIP "There's no God like Jehovah" — we're time-conscious • Heavy intros, then drop out: our standard for instant dynamics • Drop dynamics in the verses, build into the chorus • 105 BPM is the middle ground that works across all three medley songs
02
Lord I Lift Your Name on High
Checking…
Structure · New Anthem Nights "Be Lifted High" arrangementSame chord progression throughout the entire song. Variation comes from dynamics and baseline, not chords. Synth brass texture defines the feel. STRUCTURE (entering from Days of Elijah vamp) • Vamp continues (2 cycles) • "You came from heaven to earth" intro phrase ×1 • Drop into Verse 1 ("Lord I lift your name on high") — dynamics low, band drops out • "You came from heaven" chorus ×1 • Back to Verse (low dynamics again) • "You came from heaven" chorus ×2 • Straight into Blessing and Honour — no break KEY NOTES • ALWAYS drop dynamics in verse, build into chorus • Same numbers, same progression — verse just gets the "Don't know what you came to do" treatment (Anthem Nights style: build it up, let the vocal breathe) • Adlib bar after "I'm so glad you came to save us" → "Lord, I lift your name on high" — this is where the verse repeats • Seamless transition into Ancient of Days — no count-in
03
Ancient of Days / Blessing and Honour
Checking…
Structure · Standard chord progression · Definitive endingCloses the praise medley. Regular Blessing and Honour chord progression — not the "Be Lifted High" arrangement. Slowest of the three medley songs. STRUCTURE • Blessing and Honour (full verse: "Glory and power be unto the Ancient of Days, from every nation, all of creation, bow before the Ancient of Days") ×1 • "Every tongue in heaven and earth shall declare your glory" ×1 • Blessing and Honour ×1 • Chorus • "Every tongue" ×2 • "Oh Ancient of Days" ×4 → definitive ending ENDING • 1-6-7-1 chord progression — crash on the one • This is the wall the praise lands against before worship • Heavy-handed, definitive — no abrupt drop, set it up properly so we don't crash without a runway
