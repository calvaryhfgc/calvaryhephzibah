02Why we're doing this

Calvary is already streaming live on YouTube every Sunday. The goal of building out a media team is so people who can't be in the room — friends, family, grandparents in another country, someone watching from their bedroom — can still be part of what's happening. Serving on media is a meaningful way to use your time and skill to reach more people than handing out leaflets ever could.

03The kit, explained

  • Phone (camera). Mounted on a stand at the front. Runs DroidCam OBS. Replaces the old broadcast camera — better quality, faster setup.
  • Laptop. Lives in the wheely box. Runs Zoom and connects to the projector inside the church via a long cable.
  • OBS. Software that takes the camera feed and pushes it out. Used to do everything; now mostly just broadcasts the video to Zoom.
  • Control Room. Brand new. The "operator app" — where lyrics, scriptures, lower-thirds, images and announcements get pushed to the screen. Replaces the old convoluted OBS workflow.
  • Wheely box. Everything packs into one rolling case. Setup at church now takes ~15 minutes, not an hour.

04The Control Room — what we covered

  • Logging in (PIN 1234) and how the live preview lets you see exactly what's about to go on screen.
  • Pushing speaker names to a lower-third banner (e.g. "Dr Ayo · with pastor"). Saved speakers can be reused; new ones can be typed on the fly.
  • Walking through a Sunday set list — clicking each lyric line to push it. Skip ahead, jump back, blank the screen.
  • Bible verses by reference (e.g. Philippians 4:13). Single verse or ranges with Next/Previous to walk through them.
  • Pushing images, sermon thumbnails, communion graphics, and announcement cards.
  • Display settings — moving lyric position (top / middle / bottom) and adjusting distance from edge so they don't cover the singer's face.
  • The Admin view (PIN 2610) for uploading new images and saving recurring speaker names.
