Trading My Sorrows

Key of B

Praise · Opening song

Key decisions

  • Intro loops 4 times — up from 2 last week. Gives Caster clean room to find his entry. Last Sunday Vanessa came in hesitantly because the intro felt rushed.
  • Laolu's Afro groove on the verses stays in. Uncle B confirmed it worked last Sunday even though it wasn't planned.
  • Melody phrasing correction (Uncle B): "I'm trading my sor-rows" — not "I'm trading my sorrows". Same feel on "Yes Lord, yes Lord, yes, yes Lord." Caster to listen back to last Sunday's recording to lock it in. Esther to adopt the same phrasing.
Structure
  1. Intro — 2-bar groove, looped ×4
  2. Verse 1 → Verse 2 back-to-back (sorrows / sickness)
  3. Chorus (Yes Lord…) ×2
  4. Verse 1 → Verse 2 again
  5. Chorus ×4
  6. Transition straight into Lord You Are Good

Lord You Are Good

Key of B

Praise · No key change this week

Key decisions

  • No key change from Trading My Sorrows. Both songs stay in B. Removes the transition risk that bit us last week.
  • Transition: Come straight out of the final Yes Lord chorus into the Hallelujah, hallelujah chorus of Lord You Are Good.
  • Breathing moment after the first chorus — let the song breathe — then drop into the verse.

The instrumental "lick" section

  • After the first chorus, the band plays the descending melodic line together. Reference: Transformation Church version — the 1-2-3-4-3-2-1 / 2-3-4-3-2 line.
  • All instruments play the line in unison. Bass plays the harmonic version underneath.
  • Laolu: kick pattern follows the line — hits on the accents, crash on the landings.
  • Morayo: plays the line on electric. Distortion would lift it further but not required this Sunday — flag for future.
  • Play the line ×2 before returning to the chorus groove.
Structure
  1. Enter from Trading My Sorrows chorus → Chorus of Lord You Are Good
  2. Break — let it breathe
  3. Verse (Lord you are good and your mercy endureth forever / people from every nation…)
  4. Chorus
  5. Instrumental line ×2
  6. Chorus → vamp (You are good, yes you are…) — Caster leads, loop as feels right
  7. End on Lord you are good all the time / all the time / you are good

Friend of God

  • Intro: Drop the band down. Play the chorus groove of I Am a Friend of God as the intro. All instruments come in. 2 bars before Caster enters with Verse 1.

Mix note for FoH

Structure
  1. Intro — chorus groove, 2 bars
  2. Verse 1 → Verse 2 (Who am I… / Is it true…)
  3. Chorus (I am a friend of God…) ×2
  4. Verse 1 → Verse 2
  5. Chorus ×2
  6. Bridge (God Almighty…)
  7. Chorus ×2

Worship Set

Key of D

Be Magnified → I Exalt Thee · Choruses only

Key decisions

  • Key of D for both songs. Worked through C, C♯, E♭, E — landed on D as the most comfortable for Caster across both.
  • Choruses only — verses cut. Keeps the worship tight and the dynamics up.
  • No modulation. Uncle B suggested a key change between Be Magnified and I Exalt Thee to build dynamics — discussion went round and landed on: don't attempt without proper rehearsal time. Parked for future.
  • Keep Edmund's 7-1 / 7-1 / 5 movement on I Exalt Thee from last Sunday. BJ confirmed it works — worth holding onto.
Structure
  1. Be Magnified — short groove intro, Caster enters when ready
  2. Chorus ×2 — no verses
  3. Transition straight into I Exalt Thee
  4. Chorus — loop as Caster feels led, keep the 7-1/5 movement, then land
