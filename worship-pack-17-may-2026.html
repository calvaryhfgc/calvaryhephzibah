01 · The Vibe

Bold.
Declarative.

This is not a quiet sit-in-God's-presence Sunday. This is "we are 34 years in and we are louder than ever." Every song we play, every breath we take on that stage, is a sentence in a longer statement: He has been faithful, and we are saying so out loud.

01

Confident, not casual

Stand like you know why you're there. No apologetic intros, no "let's try to worship" energy. We start as if the answer is already yes.

02

Anchored, not chaotic

Bold doesn't mean loud-for-the-sake-of-it. The band locks the groove first. Vocals lift on top. Dynamics earn the explosions.

03

Together, not solo

Four vocalists is a wall, not a competition. We blend before we feature. Anyone taking a moment is sent into it by the rest.

04

Testimony, not performance

The story is 34 years of God's faithfulness. Sing like you mean it, play like you've lived it. The room will follow what's real.

One sentence to keep in your head all morning

"We are not asking the room to worship — we are leading them into a declaration that has already been true for 34 years."

04 · The Set List

Seven Songs.
One Arc.

The set is structured as a four-stage arc — Declaration into Testimony into Surrender into Sending. Each song knows its job. Hold each one's character; don't bleed every song into the same energy.

STAGE 01

Declaration

Songs 1–2. Open with confidence. State the truth.

STAGE 02

Testimony

Songs 3–4. 34 years of faithfulness. Personal.

STAGE 03

Surrender

Song 5–6. Bring the room low before the word, lift again at offering.

STAGE 04

Sending

Song 7. They leave singing this.

01
Stage 01 · Declaration · Opener

Lord You Are Good

Key: G~5 minHigh energy

We open with what we know. Anniversary Sunday begins with the line that's been true for 34 years — "Lord, you are good." This is the doorway into everything that follows.

Band

Bolaji — kick the count clean, no fill on the intro. Edmund opens with the piano hook, full chords by verse. Uncle B sits out the first 8 bars then enters with pads. Uncle Michael lock with the kick from bar 1. Morayo rhythm chops, hold off lead lines until the chorus repeats.

Vocals

Caster leads verse and chorus. Full four-part wall on "And your mercy endureth forever." Esther takes the high harmony, Uncle Shola the low, Aunty Jess mid. Save the run-ups for the second chorus.

Arrangement

Intro → V1 → Ch → V2 → Ch → Tag (×3) → Outro. Do not extend. Land it clean and move into song 2 without a gap — Caster bridges with one line of free worship.

02
Stage 01 · Declaration · Build

Way Maker

Key: E~6 minAnthem

The declaration goes up a level. "Even when I don't see it, you're working." This is the song that says: we have walked through years we didn't understand, and he was working the whole time.

Band

Start stripped — just Edmund on piano and bass. Drums enter on the second pre-chorus. Full band on the first chorus. Morayo — the lead line in the chorus is yours; play it clean, no improvising on first pass. Uncle B bring in the strings patch on the second verse.

Vocals

Caster verses solo. Whole team in on the chorus. The bridge ("Even when I don't see it…") starts spoken / breath, builds across four passes. By the fourth pass everyone is full voice.

Arrangement

Intro → V1 → Pre → Ch → V2 → Pre → Ch → Bridge (×4 builds) → Ch (big) → Tag. Caster's call: if the room is hot, extend the bridge to ×6. Watch his hand.

03
Stage 02 · Testimony

Goodness of God

Key: C~5 minTestimony

"All my life you have been faithful." This is the anniversary heartbeat. The song where the room realises this isn't just about today — it's about a long story they're all part of.

Band

Soft, intimate top. Edmund piano-led, Uncle B pads underneath. Bolaji brushes or rim only until the bridge. Uncle Michael root notes, no movement. Morayo — out for V1 and V2; enter on the bridge with sustained ambience only.

Vocals

Esther leads this one. Caster steps back to harmony. Aunty Jess and Uncle Shola in on the chorus. The bridge is where everyone lifts together — but stay under the congregation. They need to hear themselves singing it.

Arrangement

V1 → Ch → V2 → Ch → Bridge (×3) → Ch → Tag. Bridge is where the testimony lands. Hold space after the tag — Caster brings in song 4 with one spoken line.

04
Stage 02 · Testimony · Lift

Friend of God

Key: G~5 minMid-tempo lift

"Who am I, that you are mindful of me?" The most personal moment of the morning. After 34 years, we still get to call him friend. This song is small until it's not.

Band

Pick up the tempo gently from song 3. Bolaji 4-on-the-floor on the chorus, brushes on the verse. Edmund melodic rhythm. Morayo — your turn for a lead line on the second chorus, keep it singable. Uncle B Hammond/organ patch on the bridge — it should feel like a small choir entering.

Vocals

Wall vocals on every "I am a friend of God." This is a call-and-response song — let the congregation answer you. Caster calls "He calls me…" the room responds "Friend." Don't sing the response — let them.

Arrangement

V → Ch → V → Ch → Bridge → Ch (×2). Land hard on the last chorus, drop into a moment of stillness before song 5.

05
Stage 03 · Surrender · Before the Word

Great Are You Lord

Key: A~4 minSoft → swell

"It's your breath in our lungs, so we pour out our praise." The pre-word moment. We're bringing the room down before Pastor Shade speaks. They should be sitting on the edge of their seats.

Band

Strip right back. Edmund alone for the first verse. Uncle B joins with pads on the chorus. Drums (rim and hat only) and bass enter on the bridge. Morayo — sustained ambience, no rhythm parts. This is a single building wave.

Vocals

Caster verse 1 alone. Aunty Jess joins on V2. Full team on the chorus. The bridge — "It's your breath" — build across four passes from whisper to roar. End on a held note as the band drops out, then resolve into silence.

Arrangement

V → Ch → V → Ch → Bridge (×4 building) → Ch → Hold. Land softly — do not finish with a button. Final chord fades, Caster says one line of prayer, hands off to Pastor Shade.

06
Stage 03 · Offering

Trading My Sorrows

Key: E~5 minAnthem · joyful

The pivot back into celebration. After the word, this is "yes Lord" — joy as offering. The song the team has rehearsed hardest. Let it do its job.

Band

Full energy from bar 1. Bolaji — the groove is the song. Lock it and don't get fancy. Morayo — your rhythm part is the hook; everyone hangs off it. Edmund stabs on the chorus. Uncle B Rhodes on verses, organ on chorus. Uncle Michael walk on the pre-chorus.

Vocals

This is everyone's song. Trade lines on the "yes Lord, yes Lord, yes yes Lord." Caster calls verses, the wall responds. On the bridge ("I am pressed but not crushed…"), Esther takes the lead — she's been working it.

Arrangement

V → Ch → V → Ch → Bridge → Ch → Yes Lord tag (×4) → Big finish. Time it to the offering — if the buckets aren't back, extend the yes-Lord tag. Watch the back of the room.

07
Stage 04 · Sending

Every Praise

Key: F~5 minExplosive · send-off

They leave singing this. "Every praise is to our God." The last sentence of the day — and the one that walks out into the lunch hall, into the car park, into the rest of the week.

Band

Full band from the top. Bolaji — open hats throughout, big fills before each chorus. Morayo — lead line is yours, double the vocal melody on the chorus. Edmund piano stabs. Uncle B sustained chords + organ swell on the tag. Uncle Michael — walking bass on the verses, root on chorus.

Vocals

Wall vocals on every "Every praise is to our God." This is a call song — Caster calls, the room sings back. The "sing hallelujah" tag goes as long as the room wants it. Read the room — don't cut it short.

Arrangement

V → Ch → V → Ch → Bridge → Hallelujah tag (open-ended). End on the highest energy of the morning. No fade, no soft outro. Big chord, big finish, Caster sends them out with one line.
