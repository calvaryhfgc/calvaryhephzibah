The four things that matter most

Lock these in before Sunday. Everything else is detail.

1

Praise medley locks at 105 BPM. All three medley songs — Days of Elijah, Lord I Lift Your Name, Ancient of Days / Blessing and Honour — sit at 105. That's slower than we'd normally do Days of Elijah and slightly faster than we'd normally do Blessing and Honour. 105 is the middle ground that makes the medley seamless.

2

Seamless transitions in the medley — no count-ins between songs. Days of Elijah vamps into Lord I Lift Your Name. Lord I Lift Your Name flows straight into Ancient of Days. The whole medley is one ride. Drummer especially: don't count in the next song.

3

Heavy intros, then drop out. Build into the chorus, every time. This is the band discipline for the whole set. Verses are quiet, choruses are full. Don't pre-build. The dynamic gap between verse and chorus is what makes the set feel alive.

4

Worship is flexible. Praise is locked, worship breathes. Holy Spirit You Are Welcome Here · Atmosphere Shift · Spirit Break Out are Pentecost songs — Caster leads, band follows. If the room sits in something, sit with it. Don't rush to the next thing.

These Are the Days of Elijah

BPM 105 ⚠ Slower than usual
The shape

Intro: full chorus heavy-handed, then drop out. Verse 1 ("Days of Elijah") low dynamics. Chorus ("Behold He comes") ×1. Verse 2 ("Days of Ezekiel"). Chorus ×2 on the second pass. Vamp (2 cycles) → straight into Lord I Lift Your Name.

The principle

105 BPM is the Donnie McClurkin pocket — slower than we'd normally play this. Don't drift up. Skip "There's no God like Jehovah" — we're time-conscious. Heavy intro, drop out, dynamic build is the template for the whole medley.

DrumsTBC

Intro: full hits. Drop right out for verse 1 — kick only, hi-hat closed. Build through chorus. Hold 105 — don't push. Vamp at the end stays steady; no count-in into the next song.

BassBJ (if drummer found) / TBC

Drop out with drums on verse 1. Come back in on the chorus. The vamp is yours and the drummer's — keep it locked while keys and guitar fade Days of Elijah out and Lord I Lift Your Name fades in.

KeysEddie (confirming)

Heavy on the intro chorus. Pad through verse — quiet. Lift on choruses. On the vamp, start to introduce the Lord I Lift Your Name texture.

GuitarMorayo

Rhythm chords. Match the dynamic — back on verse, full on chorus. If Eddie isn't there, you carry more of the harmonic weight on the verses.

LeadUncle Caster

You set the pocket. Skip "There's no God like Jehovah" — it's a time call, not a vocal call. Land the second chorus full, then ride the vamp into Lord I Lift Your Name.

BVsEsther + (TBC × 2)

Out on verses, full on choruses. Match Caster's dynamic — when he pulls back, you pull back.

Praise medley · 2 of 3

Lord I Lift Your Name on High

BPM 105 Anthem Nights "Be Lifted High"
The shape

Coming in off the Days of Elijah vamp (no count-in). Vamp continues 2 cycles. "You came from heaven to earth" intro phrase ×1. Drop into verse 1 ("Lord I lift your name") — dynamics low, band almost out. Chorus ×1. Verse again (low). Chorus ×2. Straight into Ancient of Days — no break.

The principle

Same chord progression all the way through — verse and chorus. Variation lives in dynamics and baseline, not chords. Synth brass texture defines the feel. Drop dynamics in verse, build into chorus, every time. Adlib bar after "I'm so glad you came to save us" → back to "Lord, I lift your name on high".

KeysEddie (confirming)

You define this arrangement. Synth brass on the chorus is the signature. Pad under the verse, switch texture on the chorus. If you're not there, this song loses a lot of its identity — Morayo and Caster will need to fill that space.

BassBJ / TBC

The variation is in your baseline. Same progression, different feel between verse and chorus. Pull back on verses (long notes, root only), drive on choruses (eighths, walking).

DrumsTBC

Pull right back on the verse — kick + hi-hat. Build into chorus. Hold 105 with bass. Final chorus ×2 builds to a hand-off into Ancient of Days — no fill, just a sustained crash that resolves into the next song.

GuitarMorayo

Sustained chords. Match keys on the texture shift — verse quiet, chorus full. If Eddie isn't there, lean into chord voicing to fill the brass space.

LeadUncle Caster

The verse is intimate — let it breathe, don't push. Anthem Nights treatment on "Don't know what you came to do". Adlib bar after "I'm so glad you came to save us" sets up the verse repeat.

BVsEsther + (TBC × 2)

Out on the verses. Join Caster on the chorus. Big on the final chorus ×2 — this is the lift before we land into Ancient of Days.

Ancient of Days / Blessing and Honour

BPM 105 ⚠ Definitive ending
The shape

Standard Blessing and Honour chord progression — not the Anthem Nights arrangement. Blessing and Honour verse ×1. "Every tongue in heaven and earth" ×1. Blessing and Honour ×1. Chorus. "Every tongue" ×2. "Oh Ancient of Days" ×4 → definitive ending: 1-6-7-1 progression, crash on the one.

The principle

This is the wall the praise medley lands against. Set the ending up properly — don't crash without a runway. Heavy-handed, definitive. Slowest of the three medley songs but still locked at 105. This is the bridge moment before the room shifts into worship.

DrumsTBC

The ending is yours. Set up the 1-6-7-1 — give us a fill that signals the crash is coming. Don't surprise the band with the ending. Land the crash on the one — definitive, not abrupt.

BassBJ / TBC

Walk the 1-6-7-1 with drums on the ending. Heavy notes, full sustain. End the praise medley on the one alongside the crash.

KeysEddie (confirming)

Switch off the Anthem Nights texture — this is the Blessing and Honour arrangement. Full chords through. Hold the final 1 — sustain it as the room shifts.

GuitarMorayo

Big strums, heavy-handed for "Oh Ancient of Days" ×4. Crash on the one with the band.

LeadUncle Caster

"Oh Ancient of Days" ×4 is yours to drive — make sure the band hears the lift on each repeat. After the crash, let the room sit before you start Holy Spirit. Don't rush into worship — the gap is sacred.

BVsEsther + (TBC × 2)

Full voice through. Big on the "Oh Ancient of Days" ×4. Hold the final note with Caster.

Holy Spirit You Are Welcome Here

Flexible Caster-led · no fixed structure
The shape

Simple song, few lines — that's the point. No fixed structure: Caster leads, band supports. Use it to lift the room from praise-energy into worship-posture. It's Pentecost — leave space for the Holy Spirit to move.

The principle

Band sits underneath. Vocals carry it. If the room sits in a line, sit with the room. Don't push forward to fill silence. Silence is part of the worship.

LeadUncle Caster

You set the pace and shape. Stay in a line as long as the room is in it. Repeat a phrase if the moment calls for it. The band reads you.

KeysEddie (confirming)

Sustained pad underneath. No movement. You're holding atmosphere, not adding texture.

GuitarMorayo

Long, sustained chords or out entirely. If keys are absent, you take the pad role.

DrumsTBC

Out, or hi-hat only on the very softest touch. Don't groove this. Hold the room.

BassBJ / TBC

Out, or sustained root only. No movement.

BVsEsther + (TBC × 2)

Soft harmony following Caster. Don't lead — follow. If Caster holds a line, hold with him.

Atmosphere Shift

Phil Thompson ⚠ Bridge is the moment
The shape

Verse: "There is only one name". Chorus: "A God He's champion, He reigns forever more, forever more". Bridge: "Atmosphere shift now, change me, broken break now, Holy Spirit come down, Heaven open over us". Let it build naturally — don't rush.

The principle

The bridge is the song. Everything before it is set-up. Sit in the bridge — repeat lines if the room is in it. Morayo knows it well — lean on her on the bridge.

GuitarMorayo

You know this song. Drive the bridge — your part lifts the band when "Atmosphere shift now" comes around. Don't hold back.

LeadUncle Caster

Build through verse and chorus. Open up on the bridge. If the room is in "Heaven open over us", repeat it. Don't rush to land it.

KeysEddie (confirming)

Pad through verse and chorus. Open up texturally on the bridge — pad swells, big chords. This is where keys shine.

DrumsTBC

Quiet groove on verse. Lift on chorus. Open up properly on the bridge — toms, crashes, full kit. Settle back if Caster pulls it down for a repeat.

BassBJ / TBC

Walk the verse. Lock the chorus. Drive the bridge — eighths or quarters, full sustain.

BVsEsther + (TBC × 2)

Big on the chorus. Full voice on the bridge — "Holy Spirit come down" is everyone's line.

Worship · 3 of 3 · Closer

Spirit Break Out

William McDowell Long-form · let it stretch
The shape

We've been doing this for years — the team knows it cold. No imposed structure. Caster leads, band follows. Build, stretch, hold, repeat. Land it when Caster lands it.

The principle

It's Pentecost. Let it stretch and breathe. If the atmosphere is heavy, sit in it. Don't rush to the next thing. There is no next thing — this is the end of worship.

LeadUncle Caster

You decide how long this song lives for. If you want to stretch it, the band is with you. If you want to repeat "Spirit break out", repeat. Land it when you're ready.

KeysEddie (confirming)

Pad through. Open up on the chorus. Hold the texture if Caster sits in a line.

DrumsTBC

Watch Caster. If he lifts, you lift. If he holds, you hold. Builds and drops can repeat as many times as the room needs.

BassBJ / TBC

Solid pocket. Build with drums on the lifts. Stay underneath when Caster takes it down.

GuitarMorayo

Sustained chords through. Open up texturally on the chorus. You and keys together hold the atmosphere when Caster repeats a line.

BVsEsther + (TBC × 2)

Follow Caster's call. Full voice on the chorus. If he sits in "Spirit break out", you sit with him.

What to bring, what to remember

The small things that make Sunday smooth.

🎚

Mix priorities: Caster's voice always on top. Bass and drums locked underneath the medley. Keys (if Eddie is there) defines Lord I Lift Your Name — make sure his brass texture lands.

🤝

Praise is locked, worship breathes. In the medley, everyone is on the same map. In worship, everyone is reading Caster. If you're not sure what's next — look at Caster.

🕊

It's Pentecost. The worship section is built to leave space for the Holy Spirit to move. We're not running a programme — we're stewarding a room. If the room sits in something, sit with it.

🙏

Pray before the set. Five minutes as a band before doors open. We come off a big anniversary. The Spirit doesn't need our hype — He needs our availability.
