01 · The Four Things

Read this first

If you do nothing else, lock these in before Sunday. Everything else is detail.

1

Praise medley locks at 105 BPM. All three songs — Days of Elijah, Lord I Lift Your Name, Ancient of Days / Blessing and Honour — sit at 105. Slower than we'd normally do Days of Elijah, slightly faster than Blessing and Honour. 105 is the middle ground that makes the medley seamless.

2

Seamless transitions in the medley — no count-ins between songs. Days of Elijah vamps into Lord I Lift Your Name. Lord I Lift Your Name flows straight into Ancient of Days. The whole medley is one ride. Drummer especially: don't count in the next song.

3

Heavy intros, then drop out. Build into the chorus, every time. This is the band discipline for the whole set. Verses are quiet, choruses are full. Don't pre-build. The dynamic gap between verse and chorus is what makes the set feel alive.

4

Worship breathes. Praise is locked. Worship is not. Holy Spirit You Are Welcome Here · Atmosphere Shift · Spirit Break Out are Pentecost songs — Caster leads, band follows. If the room sits in something, sit with it. Don't rush to the next thing.

04 · Sunday Morning

What to bring

The small things that make Sunday smooth.

⏰

9am call. Setup, soundcheck, walk through the medley transitions and the bridge of Atmosphere Shift. Drummer especially needs the walk-through if confirmed late.

🎚

Mix priorities. Caster's voice always on top. Bass and drums locked underneath the medley. Keys (if Eddie is there) defines Lord I Lift Your Name — make sure his brass texture lands.

🤝

Praise is locked, worship breathes. In the medley everyone is on the same map. In worship everyone is reading Caster. If you're not sure what's next — look at him.

🕊

It's Pentecost. The worship section is built to leave space for the Holy Spirit to move. We're not running a programme — we're stewarding a room. If the room sits in something, sit with it.

🙏

Pray before the set. Five minutes as a band before doors open. We come off a big anniversary. The Spirit doesn't need our hype — He needs our availability.
