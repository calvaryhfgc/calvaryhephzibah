Per-song breakdown

1. Trading My Sorrows

🎹 Key: E (Sunday) · rehearsed in B ⏱ Tempo: ~moderate, don't rush 📍 Status: Mostly locked, dynamics still need attention

📐 Arrangement (the new shape)

  1. Intro — looped 8 times (multiple of 4) Build slowly. Drums hardly play. Bass minimal. Caster sets the count.
  2. Verse 1 — Caster alone "I'm trading my sorrows…" Esther holds back. Instruments stay really low.
  3. Verse 2 — Caster + Esther together Esther only enters here. Drums lift slightly. Still controlled.
  4. The run 4 3 2 / 2 3 4 / 1 → 7 — but simplified: drop the 2 3 so it lands cleaner. Whole band hits this together.
  5. Back into the verse — full build After the run, return to top of verse with full dynamics. This is where the song opens up. Snare comes in, kick lifts.
  6. Chorus — "Yes Lord, Yes Lord, Yes Yes Lord" Chord shape: 6 4 6 4 / 5 5 6.

🎯 The dynamics principle

Uncle Michael's earlier feedback — that the song felt rushed and too busy — wasn't a tempo problem. It was a dynamics problem. We were filling every bar instead of letting the song breathe. The new arrangement fixes that by building deliberately: nothing → Caster alone → Caster + Esther → full band on the run.

👥 Per-part guidance

Drums BJ

The big note. Drums have been coming in too early. Hold back through the intro and verse 1 — be barely audible. Lift on verse 2. Snare and kick come after the run, on the second time round. Follow Uncle Michael's slap pattern on the intro — light cymbal/tom work, not too much.

Bass Uncle Michael

Don't really come in until Esther comes in (or until the run). The bass entry is a dynamic moment — let it be one. We worked out a movement on the bass for the chorus.

Vocals Esther

Don't enter on verse 1. Caster does the first verse alone. You come in for verse 2. Match Caster's line — what you're singing should be in line with his. Mic closer when you sing.

Lead Vocal Caster

You're leading this whole thing — the band is counting on you to call the structure. Lock in the intro count, signal the verse, signal the run. Strong start to the intro.

Guitar Morayo

Joining tomorrow morning. Note: our Trading My Sorrows movement is slightly different to what you'll remember — we'll walk you through the change at the 9am setup.

What's working: When we strip it down and play together — really together — it sounds completely different. Someone literally said "it makes such a difference." That's the version we want on Sunday.

Open / watch for

  • Transposing the rehearsed B back to E for Sunday — make sure the chord numbers translate cleanly
  • Caster's intro count needs to be confident every time — don't second-guess mid-loop
  • If we're too loud, Liv (next door) will let us know — the kick was shaking the frames in her studio. Keep volumes down at venue.

2. Lord You Are Good

Straight in — no "Yes, yes, Lord" intro chant. We talked about it and decided to drop it, like we did last time. Goes directly into "We worship You / Hallelujah."

The "We worship You" chorus is played twice as the intro before any singer comes in. By that point the band has already taken us through the chorus once, so it's effectively a two-round musical intro to settle into.

3. Friend of God

📍 Status: Cued by BJ's Every Praise underplay → flows into Every Praise as 4th song

📐 Arrangement

Standard chorus / verse / chorus / pre-chorus / chorus shape. The song is short — verse and chorus only, really. Each song in the praise set is around 2–3 minutes, so there's room to breathe.

🔗 Transition into Every Praise

This is the new piece. BJ plays Every Praise underneath the back end of Friend of God as a cue — building it in the background while Caster is still on "I'm a friend of God." That underplay is the signal: we're moving into Every Praise next.

Caster's job is to feel the build, take Friend of God to its end ("I'm a friend of God" twice), then we pause briefly and drop into Every Praise as the 4th praise song.

👥 Per-part guidance

Lead Vocal Caster

"I'm a friend of God" sung twice at the end. Listen for BJ's Every Praise underplay coming in — that's your cue we're transitioning. Take Friend of God to its end, brief pause, then call us into Every Praise.

Drums BJ

You're carrying the transition. Start playing Every Praise underneath the back end of Friend of God as a build — that's the cue for the band. Don't make it a full bridge inside Friend of God; it's a signal that Every Praise is coming next.

Bass Uncle Michael

Pick up on BJ's Every Praise underplay and start moving with him into the new feel. The transition is the moment.

All

If you hear the Every Praise feel underneath, lean into it. We're not stopping the praise set — we're building it.

Open / watch for

  • One muddle in rehearsal — BJ called out "God Almighty" by mistake instead of "God My Saviour." Sorted, but worth being deliberate with song-name calls on Sunday so no one comes in on the wrong song.
